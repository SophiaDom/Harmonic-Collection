STUYVESANT PARK





Opening in 1850, and developed in the 1930s, leaving it looking very similar to its current status. The cast-iron fence has been maintained since its first opening. The trees populating this park are well over 100 years old, becoming history themselves. Over time, statues, benches, tables, and fountains were added, but the main character of these twin parks are their English Elms.


ENGLISH ELMS

The winding branches, leaning trunks and great height remind me I'm in a new place. Immitating the skyscrapers.
