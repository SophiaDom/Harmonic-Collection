NORTH WOODS































The North Woods are located in the mid-upper west side of Centeral Park. It's 40 acres of woods landscapes designed by Federick Law Olmsted and Calvert Vaux. Although manmade these landscapes are built off of the natural topography of hills and rocks with planted trees and vegetation as well as manmade bodies of water, small waterfalls and bridges.

This area of the park is accessable during all seasons of the year. Other open feilds such as Sheep's Meadow and the Great Lawn close seasonally to maintain the grass' condition for events hosted and visitor safety.

The Ravine area has paths on either side of the "Loch" body of water consisting of three waterfalls. This is the only part in the city where city noise and cityscapes are blocked out. Visiting the Norht Woods acts as an escape from urban life. Unkown to tourist it is mostly enjoyed by New Yorkers.
