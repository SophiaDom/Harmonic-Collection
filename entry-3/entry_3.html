CENTRAL PARK

Central Park was made with the intent to provide New York City with green space but also to put New York City on international radar. In 1853, the government bought land from 59th-106th Streets between Fifth and Eighth Avenues. Seneca Village between 82nd and 89th Streets was a significant community of African-American property owners. This land was populated, swampy, rocky land, yet they excavated, "...nearly 5 million cubic yards of stone, earth, and topsoil, built 36 bridges and arches, and constructed 11 overpasses over the transverse roads. They also planted 500,000 trees, shrubs, and vines. The landscapes are manmade and all built by hand."
