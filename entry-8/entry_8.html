Union Square's Climate Clock



Climate Clock has locations around the world, one being in NYC's Union Square. The Clock displays a live countdown until the date where climate change is irreversable; specifically the critical time window to reach zero emmissions". The countdown can be extended through lessening carbon emissions and reflects current events and disasters allowing fluxuation in it's span. This clock brings awareness to the climate crisis and gives a visual of the time passing without enough action.

The Green Market



Each Monday, Wednesday, Friday and Saturday year-round Union Square's Greenmarket fills the North and West corners of the park. Local artists and local farmers, fishers and bakers sell their products from the Tri-State County. This market below the climate clock supports the climate crisis and attempts to engage even the city's citizens into organic foods and products, supprting local buisnesses and rejecting capitalism.

Historical Rememberence



There are many monuments for historical figures throughout the park including, Abe Lincoln, Mohandas Ghandi, and George Washington. The park itself also has a history being designated as a National Historic Landmark in 1997.
